2 Scope and Objectives of Function  
Scope and Objectives of Use Case 
Scope 
The proposed IoT platform aims to digitalize and automate a supermarket 
through a microservices architectur. The system integrates smart shopping 
carts, real-time inventory management with autonomous robots, sensor for  
monitoring environmental conditions and Telegram bots for both customers and 
store staff. 
Objective(s) 
The primary objectives are to automate product management within smart 
shopping carts and smart shelves, streamline the checkout process, and 
provide store staff with real-time control over operations. Additionally, the 
platform monitors customer behavior (dwell time), sales trends, and 
environmental anomalies. Inventory updates are managed autonomously by 
Smart Shelves and Robots to prevent stockouts. 
Domain(s) Smart Building, Smart Retail. 
Stakeholder(s) Supermarket customers, Store staff, Warehouse managers, Store management 
and administration, Cashier. 
Short description 
The Smart Market platform automates the retail experience by integrating RFID-
enabled Smart Carts that synchronize directly with a User Telegram Bot, 
enabling real-time shopping list updates and generating code for the checkout. 
On the operational side, Smart Shelves  automatically detect low stock and 
trigger Autonomous Robots to retrieve products from the warehouse  while a 
Staff Bot  allows employees to manage alerts and finalize transactions. The 
ecosystem is completed by smart sensors for monitoring environmental 
conditions (temperature, crowd levels) and an analytics module for tracking sales 
trends and customer dwell time, all sup ported by a microservices architecture 
using REST and MQTT protocols. 
 
Key Features Summary: 
ò Smart Cart with RFID technology connected to the User Bot. 
ò Staff Telegram Bot for checkout operations and alert management. 
ò Automatic low-stock alerting and real -time database updates sent by 
Smart Shelves and processed by Autonomous Robots. 
ò Environmental sensors visualizable by both customers and staff. 
ò Communication is based on REST and MQTT protocols.

4 Complete description of the system  
The proposed IoT platform for Smart Market follows the microservices designing pattern. It also exploits two 
communication paradigms: i) publish/subscribe based on MQTT protocol and ii) request/response based on 
REST Web Services. 
In this context, 13 actors have been identified and introduced in the following:  
ò The Message Broker provides asynchronous communication based on the publish/subscribe 
approach. It exploits the MQTT protocol.  
ò The Market Catalog works both as device and service registry for the entire system. 
It acts as a REST provider, exposing Web Services queried by all other actors to retrieve 
heterogeneous information. 
ò The Device Connector (Raspberry Pi Connector) integrates the platform Raspberry Pi boards. 
It collects environmental data (temperature and humidity) from the market and fridge areas. We will 
use two Raspberry Pi connector: one for the fridge and one for the market environment. 
Connections: 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to register itself with the Market Catalog. 
o Acts as an MQTT Publisher to send real-time environmental data to the Message Broker. 
o Acts as a REST Provider to expose live data directly to the Node-RED dashboard. 
 
ò The Thingspeak Adaptor receives raw measurements and analytics reports, uploading them to the 
ThingSpeak cloud platform. 
Connections: 
o Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive data from the Broker. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to push data to ThingSpeak (Third-party platform).

o Acts as a REST Provider to expose historical time-series data to the Node-RED dashboard. 
ò Thingspeak is a third-party software (https://thingspeak.com/) that provides REST Web Services. It 
is an open-data platform for the Internet of Things to store and visualize data (through plots). 
ò Nodered is a dashboard to retrieve data and visualize them. It retrieves both historical and live data. 
Connections: 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to pull historical data from the ThingSpeak Adaptor. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to pull live environmental data from the Device Connector. 
 
ò The Smart Cart is composed by an RFID sensor and a smart touch screen (Cart Telegram Bot). 
It detects items placed in the cart and updates the virtual shopping session. 
Connections: 
o RFID Sensor: Acts as an MQTT Publisher, sending the detected Item IDs to the Broker. 
o Cart Bot: Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive the wish list from the User Bot and 
updates from the RFID sensor on which product was added to the cart. 
Acts as a REST Consumer to query product details (name, price) from the Catalog. 
Acts as MQTT subscriber to publish the payment code to the Broker. 
 
ò User Telegram Bot is the customer interface for catalogue browsing and checkout. 
Allows users to create wishlists, pair with a Smart Cart, visualize the Nodered dashboard and 
process payments. 
Connections: 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to retrieve the product list from the Catalog. 
o Acts as an MQTT Publisher to send wishlist data to the cart. 
o Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive the final payment confirmation and QR code. 
 
ò Staff Telegram Bot is the management tool for store employees. Allows staff to monitor crowd 
levels, robot status, visualize the Nodered dashboard and handle alerts. 
Connections: 
o Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive alerts (low stock, high temperature) generated by 
the Alert System. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to manually update the Catalog (e.g., changing product prices or 
stock). 
ò The Smart Shelf is an IoT device capable of monitoring product availability in real-time thanks to 
RFID technology. It performs continuous local polling of the tags present on the shelf. Ideally, it 
applies a "Report by Exception" logic: the local controller compares the tag list at time T with time T-
1. 
Connections: 
o If an ID disappears (removal) or appears (addition), it acts as an MQTT Publisher, sending 
an event (to the Broker to notify the Alert System. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to register itself with the Market Catalog. 
 
ò The Robot service manages the autonomous restocking agents. It waits for specific restocking 
commands generated by the Alert System when stock is low. It coordinates the robot's movement to 
retrieve products from the warehouse and refill the shelves. 
Connections: 
o Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive specific "Restock" commands from the Alert 
System. 
o Acts as an MQTT Publisher to broadcast its status (e.g., "Moving", "Refilling") to the Staff 
Bot. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to query the Catalog for product warehouse locations.

ò The Alert System acts as the central operational brain of the supermarket. It verifies real-time data 
from environmental sensors and processes events from Smart Shelves. It updates the inventory in 
the Catalog upon receiving RFID events and checks safety thresholds (e.g., fridge temperature, 
minimum stock levels). If a threshold is breached, it triggers alerts to the Staff Bot or sends mission 
commands to the Robot Service. 
Connections: 
o Acts as an MQTT Subscriber to receive sensor data (temperature) and shelf events (RFID 
updates). 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to update stock levels in the Catalog and retrieve product details. 
o Acts as an MQTT Publisher to send alarms to the Staff Bot and restocking commands to the 
Robot Service. 
 
ò Analytics Service provides a historical view of the market. It processes transaction data to calculate 
crowd levels, shopping habits, and sales history. 
Connections: 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to retrieve transaction logs from the Catalog. 
o Acts as a REST Consumer to push the processed reports directly to the ThingSpeak 
Adaptor via REST APIs for visualization.
